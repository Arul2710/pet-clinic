Why Vaccinate

Benefits of Pet Vaccination

Vaccinations are one of the most effective ways to protect your pet from serious diseases.

Disease Prevention

Vaccines stimulate your pet's immune system to produce antibodies, providing protection against deadly diseases like distemper, parvovirus, and rabies.

Cost-Effective Care

Preventing disease through vaccination is far more affordable than treating serious illnesses. Save on costly emergency veterinary bills.

Herd Immunity

When most pets in a community are vaccinated, it reduces disease spread and protects even unvaccinated animals through herd immunity.

Legal Compliance

Rabies vaccination is legally required in most states and countries. Stay compliant with local laws while protecting your pet and community.

Zoonotic Protection

Some pet diseases can be transmitted to humans. Vaccinating your pet protects your entire family from zoonotic diseases like rabies.

Boarding & Travel

Most kennels, daycares, and grooming facilities require up-to-date vaccinations. Traveling with your pet also requires documented vaccine history.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions about pet vaccinations? Find answers to common concerns below.

Vaccinations are essential for protecting your pet from serious, potentially fatal diseases. They work by stimulating your pet's immune system to produce antibodies against specific viruses and bacteria. Core vaccines protect against widespread, highly contagious diseases like distemper, parvovirus, and rabies. Vaccinating your pet also helps protect the broader animal community through herd immunity and is often required by law for rabies.

Most pets experience no side effects or only mild, temporary reactions. Common mild side effects include slight discomfort at the injection site, mild fever, decreased appetite, or lethargy for 24-48 hours. Serious allergic reactions are extremely rare but can include facial swelling, vomiting, or difficulty breathing. Our veterinarians monitor your pet after vaccination and are equipped to handle any reactions. Always inform your vet about any previous vaccine reactions.

Vaccination costs vary depending on the type of vaccine and your location. Core vaccines typically range from $20-$45 per shot, while combination vaccines like DHPP or FVRCP may cost $50-$90. Rabies vaccination is usually $20-$40. We offer affordable wellness packages that bundle vaccinations with annual checkups at a discounted rate. Most pet insurance plans cover routine vaccinations. Contact our clinic for a detailed price list and package options.

Generally, vaccines should only be given to healthy pets. If your pet is showing signs of illness such as fever, vomiting, diarrhea, or respiratory symptoms it's best to wait until they've fully recovered. A weakened immune system may not respond optimally to vaccination and could increase the risk of side effects. Our veterinarians perform a thorough health examination before administering any vaccine to ensure your pet is in good health and ready for immunization.

Core vaccines are recommended for all pets regardless of lifestyle. For dogs: DHPP (distemper, hepatitis, parainfluenza, parvovirus) and rabies. For cats: FVRCP (feline viral rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, panleukopenia) and rabies. Non-core vaccines are given based on your pet's lifestyle, exposure risk, and geographic location. Examples include: Bordetella (kennel cough), Leptospirosis, Lyme disease, Canine Influenza, and Feline Leukemia (FeLV). Our vets will recommend non-core vaccines based on your pet's specific needs.
