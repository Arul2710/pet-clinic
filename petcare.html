Diet & Nutrition

Pet Nutrition Advice

Proper nutrition is vital for your pet's health at every life stage. Get evidence-based dietary recommendations from our veterinary team.

Canine Nutrition

Dietary Guidelines for Dogs

  • High-Quality Protein ” Choose real meat (chicken, beef, fish, lamb) as the first ingredient. Dogs thrive on animal-based proteins for muscle and energy.
  • Healthy Fats ” Omega-3 and Omega-6 fatty acids from fish oil and flaxseed support a shiny coat, healthy skin, and brain function.
  • Complex Carbohydrates ” Sweet potatoes, brown rice, and oats provide sustained energy and dietary fiber for digestive health.
  • Portion Control ” Follow feeding guidelines based on weight, age, and activity level. Obesity is the most common preventable health issue in dogs.
Avoid grapes, raisins, chocolate, onions, garlic, and xylitol ” these are toxic to dogs.
Feline Nutrition

Dietary Guidelines for Cats

  • Obligate Carnivore ” Cats require meat-based diets. They need taurine, an essential amino acid found only in animal tissue, for heart and eye health.
  • Wet Food Benefits ” Canned food provides moisture crucial for urinary tract health. Cats have a low thirst drive and often don't drink enough water.
  • Protein-Rich Diet ” Look for high-protein, low-carbohydrate foods that mimic a cat's natural prey diet. Avoid fillers like corn and wheat.
  • Feeding Schedule ” Cats prefer small, frequent meals. Free-feeding dry food can lead to obesity. Measure portions and consider puzzle feeders.
Avoid onions, garlic, chocolate, raw eggs, raw fish, and milk ” many cats are lactose intolerant.

Life Stage Nutrition

Puppy, adult, and senior formulas tailored to your pet's changing nutritional needs at every age.

Food Allergies

Elimination diets and hypoallergenic options for pets with food sensitivities, skin issues, or digestive problems.

Supplements

Dental Health

Pet Dental Care

Dental disease is one of the most common health problems in pets, affecting over 80% of dogs and cats by age three. Good oral hygiene is essential for overall health.

Dental Exams

Thorough oral examinations to check for plaque, tartar, gingivitis, periodontal disease, and oral masses.

Professional Cleanings

Ultrasonic scaling, polishing, and fluoride treatment performed under anesthesia for a thorough, stress-free cleaning.

Extractions & Surgery

Advanced dental procedures including extractions, oral surgery, and treatment of fractured or infected teeth.

Emergency Care

When to Seek Emergency Care

Recognizing a pet emergency can save your companion's life. If you notice any of these signs, contact us or visit an emergency vet immediately.

Breathing Difficulties

Labored breathing, excessive panting, coughing, gagging, or blue-tinged gums (cyanosis) indicate a respiratory emergency requiring immediate attention.

Trauma & Injuries

Hit by a car, falls from height, bite wounds from animal fights, broken bones, or any visible injury needs immediate veterinary assessment.

Poisoning & Toxins

Ingestion of chocolate, grapes, xylitol, rat poison, lilies (cats), human medications, or household chemicals. Bring the packaging if possible.

Severe Vomiting / Diarrhea

Persistent vomiting, diarrhea lasting more than 24 hours, blood in vomit or stool, or suspected foreign body ingestion (especially string or toys).

Birthing Complications

Prolonged labor (over 2 hours between puppies/kittens), visible distress, green or black discharge, or a retained fetus requires emergency intervention.

Urinary Blockage

Straining to urinate with little or no output, crying in the litter box, blood in urine, or abdominal pain ” especially common in male cats. Life-threatening.
